Resources to Dig Deeper into the E-Waste Problem

Want to better understand the global impact of e-waste and how electronic waste is (mis)managed around the world? Here’s a curated list of trusted resources to help you dig deeper.

International organizations

  • United Nations Institute for Training and Research (UNITAR)G Co-author of the Global E-Waste Monitor, one of the most comprehensive reports on global electronic waste trends.
  • Basel Convention (UNEP): Offers research, reports and initiatives on the topic of electronic waste
  • International Telecommunication Union (ITU): A UN agency working to promote sustainable electronics management and digital inclusion around the world.
  • Basel Action Network (BAN): It monitors and prevents the illegal trade of electronic waste

Documentaries and Multimedia Content

  • Global E-Waste Statistics Partnership: An interactive platform providing official data and annual reports on e-waste generation and management globally.
  • "The Global Cost of Electronic Waste" – The Atlantic: A powerful article that explores how e-waste is often illegally exported to developing countries, causing environmental and human harm.
  • iFixit – Right to Repair Movement: A platform advocating for the right to repair. Offers free repair guides and resources to extend the life of your devices and reduce e-waste.

Events and Initiatives on Electronic Sustainability

Participating in events and initiatives is a concrete way to contribute to change and learn about real solutions to the e-waste problem.

International Events:

  • International E-Waste Day (14 october): A day dedicated to raising awareness on the problem
  • E-Waste World Conference & Expo: One of the leading conferences dedicated to the electronic recycling industry, circularity and sustainable innovation.
  • Green Electronics Summit: International event that brings together companies, NGOs and institutions to discuss the future of sustainable electronics.
  • Tech for Good Global Summit: Includes sessions on the life cycle of digital devices and their environmental impact. Involves experts from around the world.

Local Initiatives:

  • Restart Parties : Community events where citizens bring broken devices and learn how to fix them with volunteer technicians. They can be held in schools, libraries or public spaces.
  • WEEE Collection Days : Initiatives promoted by municipalities or local cooperatives to collect electronic waste in a safe and responsible way.
  • Repair and Upcycling Workshops :Practical workshops (often organized by maker spaces or environmental associations) to learn how to disassemble, reuse and reinvent electronic devices.
  • Educational projects in schools on sustainable management of devices
